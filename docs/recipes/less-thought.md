# **Less-Thought: a General Art-Recipe for Joyous E-Lit**

David Jhave Johnston   
AI-Narrative Postdoctoral Researcher / Ai-Narrative Researcher (Centre for Digital Narrative)  
University of Bergen

### **Background**

I’m better at spontaneous meals: thoughtless improvs made from leftovers without a recipe. The final output art-dish may look like it was planned (conceptualized, thought-through, thoroughly understood, based on a rigorous literary/dietary analysis); but that’s an artefact, a mere appearance, a symptom of a playful autonomy, of random ricocheting materials converging within a joyous caring flexible open inquisitive resilient exploratory freshness. That is to say, thinking less is effective.

The following general abstract art-recipe is generalized for anyone anywhere at any time. It is followed by a set of specific example art-meals made using the general art-recipe-principle. Though the example-meal-chronology begins in pre-web-era (just to establish that the recipe is non-specific to any tech), it ends with a focus on AI and an emphasis on the necessity for spontaneous compassionate joy amid anthropogenic extinction entropy. Each example-art-meal very briefly also reveals how chance circumstances align in conjunction with emergent tech and vague premonitions to result in an inconceivable feast. Beginning inconceivably, ideas evolve into coherence if explored.

And what motivates the necessity for an *abstract general* recipe? Given the rapid hyper-kinetic ecosystem of frothy \#genAI software-app-obsolescence that is continually germinating, churning, mutating, and modulating the imaginative microbiome of humanity-as-a-whole, it seems appropriate to consider asking: what are the general principles that go into a *good* recipe for e-lit? 

Basically, the archetypal ingredients are as follows: Freshness (find a technology that just emerged, even if it’s an old tech that just emerged as new for you). Intuition (the gut needs to be hungry yet happy, listen to it, learn from it). Joy (a simple innocence arising as love deserves to be fed). A constraint (setting the limits that allow for optimal nourishment, putting the whipped cream into a bowl, establishing a ritual time). Spontaneity (no need for thought, if you feel the joy leaping up, act\!). Reverence (all is one: your body is just an ecosystem connected with other ecosystems within an edible ecosystem). Play (without goal or critique, non-competitively, manifesting *your* optimal creativity, comparisons are not so helpful). Relaxation (because literature is all just good-enuff suchness, ripe with a reverent relaxation that emerges after a delicious meal).

The following example online e-lit art-meals all arose from this general recipe-ingredient framework: *Big Book* (1992-95) [https://vimeo.com/12858620](https://vimeo.com/12858620), *Nomad Lingo* (2000) [https://glia.ca/2000\_NomadLingo/](http://glia.ca/2000_NomadLingo/), *Sooth* (2005) [https://glia.ca/2005\_Sooth/](http://www.glia.ca/2005_Sooth/), *MUPs* (2012) [https://glia.ca/2012/mups/](http://glia.ca/2012/mups/), *ReRites* (2019) [https://glia.ca/rerites/](http://glia.ca/rerites/), *Identity Upgrade* (2023) [https://glia.ca/2023/IdentityUpgrade](https://glia.ca/2023/IdentityUpgrade), *Hallucinations are Almost all You Need* (2024) [https://glia.ca/2024/Hallucinations/](http://glia.ca/2024/Hallucinations/), *AI-Artist* (August 13 \- 28th, 2024\) [https://glia.ca/2024/ai-artist/](https://glia.ca/2024/ai-artist/), *Messages to Humanity* (Nov 2024\) [https://glia.ca/2024/messages/](https://glia.ca/2024/messages/) 

Basically, the message is simple: set aside thought, trust intuition, play until the work begins.

# **An Abstract Recipe for *Joyful* E-Lit Creation**

### **Ingredients**

* Freshness  
* Intuition  
* Joy  
* A constraint (or two)  
* Spontaneity  
* Reverence  
* Play  
* Relaxation

---

### **Method**

1. **Begin with Intuition**  
    Does the body authentically want to say something? Is it hungry?

    To make something? If yes, let it. If no, do something anyway, just for practice.  
    Trust the tiny thought that suggests you do something.   
    Write down any & all words that arise in the mind.  
2. **Add Intention**  
    Harmless. Gracious. Compassionate. Caring.  
    Set the intention to not harm. Be of benefit.

3. **Select Fresh Ingredients**  
    Technology that feels fresh might be new, might be old.  
    Select what feels right to you. Tools. Folders. A laptop. Talking with an AI.  
    Any cybernetic situation will do if approached with the right state of mind:  
    Open. Kind. Gentle. Pristine. Tender. Radiant. Pulsing. Reverential.

4. **Listen, Then Begin**

    Be quiet. Very quiet. Silent.  
    If intuition returns an echo—begin.  
    Let it evolve. Activate energetic resources. Trust it.  
5. **Introduce Constraint**  
    A constraint can guide spontaneity.  
    A constraint can be simple: such as, do this every day for one hour for one year.  
    Just like a path you can leave it if you wish. If you miss a day, begin again.   
6. **Observe Without Interference**  
    Critique may obstruct capacity.  
    Observe internalized representations without reactivity.  
    How ephemeral they are. Insert them into the work as necessary.

7. **Check the Intuitional Ferment**  
    Return to the belly. That’s where the joy is cooking.   
    The gut is where the work is born. Trust the gut intuition.  
    Taste the heart’s song. Celebrate yourself as an ecosystem.  
8. **Bake Gently**  
    Take one neural network. One human.  
    Stir gently together. Iterate until warm.  
    Avoid overcooking. Delete the extraneous.

9. **Do Not Get Hung Up**  
    Don’t critique it as it takes shape.  
    Would you critique a baby?    
    Do you critique yourself as you shit?  
    Nourish the event. Let it spontaneously arise.  
    Without necessity, let go of doubts and judgments.  
10. **Add Joy**  
     Joy cannot be repeated, but it often loops.  
     And all joy is one joy. A singular intensity.  
     To be joyful: forgive everything. Just drop it.  
     Forget time, limitation, deadlines, productivity, and rules.

11. **Serve with Care & Play**  
     Care is the plate. Play is the cutlery.  
     Non-competitive, open, simple, exquisite, subtle.  
     Be generous. Offer portions appropriate for flourishing.

You are not the doer.

Identity is a dream. 

Don’t take it too seriously.

Become an empty conduit that transmits joy.

## A Few Art-Meals Made with the Preceding *General Recipe* (from an almost-elder’s extensive archive)

The reason I include these stale potentially-inedible items from the archives (residual nourishment from banquets of old) is to dissuade today’s youth of the sense that they need to have an idea to begin with. No\! Just grab whatever’s nearby, go with the first impulse, start making. When the joy rises, ride the tide. Radical emptiness is a very effective teacher. Opportunities ripen at the interstice of tech and events. E-lit is a meal best served fresh, free from the rigid conflated architecture of conventional fine-dining. These examples all share one characteristic: there was no plan, just an energy seed intuition germination inviting activity to explore. After the first frenzy creates the sketch, there’s still plenty of time to obsess over garnish and plating service. 

### Big Book (1992-95)

[https://vimeo.com/12858620](https://vimeo.com/12858620) “In the 1990s I found an accountant's ledger in a basement puddle at a friend's apt. on Wabash Ave in Toronto. Over 6 years, I filled it with typographic visual poetry. I eventually termed these works "plough-ems" in reference (or reverence) to the ploughed fields of the prairies where the lines of the earth twist like text released from linearity.” 

**Recipe Notes:** No one told me to do this. No grant. No concept. Just an OCD style preoccupation with writing for hours every day into a grimy beaten up old ledger. Exhibited once. Still have it. Super fun. No purpose. A soup served to the wind. 

### Nomad Lingo (2000) 

[https://glia.ca/2000\_NomadLingo/](http://glia.ca/2000_NomadLingo/) “​​NomadLingo was the first Flash web-work I ever posted online. (Preceded by the now lost 1997's html gif work: NomadSoup.) NomadLingo was initially hosted by the early net-art site www.year01.com founded by friends Camille Turner and Michael Alstad who were gracious enough to offer me server space before I really knew what a server was or what compression meant. The previous year I had made a gif that was several hundred megabytes.”

**Recipe Notes:** I didn’t know anything about code or software when I started making this in 1998\. A roommate had downloaded an illegal crack of Macromedia suite (including Flash, Fireworks, Dreamweaver); he didn’t know what the software was for. I didn’t know what a browser was, or a server. Nor anyone who was making websites until Camille and Michael started one. Through devoted ignorance and extended play I (like so many others in that open sharing time) learned jpg, swf, ftp, html and key frames. Much joy mingled with mega frustration. Ignorance made delicious mingled with a community of minds. Friends with open hearts offered helpful utensils as needed. Thinking like a baby, thoughtlessly without manners, learning sprouted. For some reason, maybe because I admired Tehching Hsieh’s five “One Year Performances”, I decided to post a set of works every month. There was no plan. No guide. Just a lot of borrowed code. And again super fun. 

### Sooth (2005) 

[https://glia.ca/2005\_Sooth/](http://glia.ca/2005_Sooth/) “For *Sooth* In 2005, I programmed flocks of poetic phrases to fly over online video. Phrases could be triggered by the user clicking, and were constrained within regions. A sound was mapped to each phrase and reflected its position within the space (panning as the phrase flew from side to side; decreasing/increasing in volume relative to size). All of this was programmatically defined; hard coded by me as regulatory behavior. It had some randomness inherent within it, but was basically choreographed by human code and user interaction.”

**Recipe Notes:** The work description leaves out a crucial item. I had just fallen in love. Seriously. Belovedly in love. What better ingredient for e-lit than love? Include it in all your recipes. Stir it into the design: self-love (that gentleness with the actuality of what is here embodied) and other-love (building an interface that invites the seemingness to digest what is here implemented). Also, just to say, this is another work with no plan. Just a set of tiny sketches launched into a sea of flash, with a sigh of love. 

### MUPs (2012) 

[https://glia.ca/2012/mups/](http://glia.ca/2012/mups/) “1260 audio poems from the PennSound archives in a user-controllable auto-splicing web-interface”

**Recipe Notes:** I can hear someone thinking to themselves ‘MUPs is so conceptual’. Yes, in final form. But it was inspired by a simple whimsical frustration because I wanted to be able to listen to many of the amazing poetry performances on the PennSound website ([https://writing.upenn.edu/pennsound/](https://writing.upenn.edu/pennsound/)) without having to press a button every time a track finished, or navigate to the next page, or wait for one track to finish before starting another. Interface angst gave birth to an intuition that all of the files could be housed in a single interface. So I scraped the mp3 files, built an interface to play them, then realized Flash could do crude mashup remixes by analyzing the mp3 bytecode for silence points to splice at. Once the interface was functioning, I sent it to PennSound, and asked for permission to publish; it was given due to the profound open generosity of the inestimable Charles Bernstein ([https://writing.upenn.edu/pennsound/x/Bernstein.php](https://writing.upenn.edu/pennsound/x/Bernstein.php)) and the encouragement of the resonant Christopher Funkhouser ([https://web.njit.edu/\~funkhous/](https://web.njit.edu/~funkhous/)).

### ReRites (2019) 

[https://glia.ca/rerites/](http://glia.ca/rerites/) “A.I. \+ Human poetry. Generated by a computer. Edited by a human. 05.2017 \- 05.2018. One book a month.”

**Recipe Notes:** Again, I can hear someone again thinking to themselves ‘ReRites is so conceptual’. Perhaps, but not at birth. In 2012, I was gainfully employed as a professor in Hong Kong. I began to be aware that AI capacity was getting interesting; I enrolled in a data science course for corporate quants; it took us 3 classes just to get the core git dependencies installed; and the concepts of a model being trained on a data corpus using linear regression felt like an alien language. My fellow-classmates were mathematicians who had been sent to monetize corporate data-mining. I meekly told them I wanted to write poems.

Meanwhile, AI research begins to boil, Fei-Fei Li launched ImageNet in 2007; October 2012 AlexNet wins the ImageNet competition with a convolutional neural net; 2013 Word2vec; 2014 GANs introduced; 2015, TensorFlow beta launched and Google published the blog post “Inceptionism: Going Deeper into Neural Networks” (https://research.google/blog/inceptionism-going-deeper-into-neural-networks/), introducing DeepDream; 2017, Pytorch beta. 

Aug 2016: I received a TitanX GPU through the NVIDIA Academic Grant Program. Dec. 2016: I quit my job in Hong Kong. Jan. 2017: I am no longer gainfully employed. I begin meditating. 

And I am appalled yet impressed by the AI output emerging from tweaked pytorch-beta word-language models I’ve trained on a custom corpus of poetry. So two months before the Transformer architecture is introduced, when Vaswani et al. published “Attention Is All You Need” in May 2017 (https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762), I begin to get up at 5am every day and edit AI-generated poems for a couple hours. “It is 6 am. It’s silent. The internet is off. Mind is hammer. I carve.” It is a delicious process: contemplative, soft, gooey, lush, delightful. Because I like the notion of constraints, I decided to do it for a year. Every few weeks or so I update the corpus, code some  changes, and train another model. Training the model usually takes 72 hours, 3 or more days 24h/day on the TitanX GPU. If I’ve made a mistake or overcook it into a vanishing gradient or overfit, the result is a collapsed souffle of nonsense. 

On March 9, 2018, after 9 months of editing, I realized that grabbing screengrabs of the editing might be nice. A video archive of the process emerges[^1]. 

At some point in 2018, someone suggests I checkout *Anteism Books*[^2]; coincidentally, it turns out their printing press is 2 blocks from my home. I visited. Harley and Ryan from *Anteism* liked the project; I liked them. They suggested Anteism will publish it. I say ok. We never have any sort of contractual agreement except verbal gratitude and mutual respect. They spend a lot of money and time pushing the boxset print through iterations. When they get an exhibit space a block away, together we conceptualize and set up a *ReRites* overhead video-projection onto boxset experiment. Then they agree to publish the *ReRites* paperback. And Stephanie Strickland with her incredible eagle eye and fastidious insight meticulously edits the issue. Thanks Anteism\! Thanks Stephanie\! 

Again, let me emphasize: no plan, no concept, no idea, no grant, no contracts. Just a process emerging. The world is food.

## Conclusion: and now I’m over the word-count for a recipe in this book, and besides you get the idea…

Each of the following projects follows the same pattern. A technological opportunity emerges, a vague premonition tickles enteric neurons. Thoughtlessly as if impelled by the instantaneous weight of a sourceless wonder, a hungry playful improvised sudden joy creates … 

* *Identity Upgrade* (2023) [https://glia.ca/2023/IdentityUpgrade](https://glia.ca/2023/IdentityUpgrade) : begun on Oct 7, 2023 as a distraction from witnessing the genocide-by-apartheid live-stream.   
* *Hallucinations are Almost all You Need* (2024) [http://glia.ca/2024/Hallucinations/](http://glia.ca/2024/Hallucinations/) : originated from a long list of links about AI that I thought might be more fun to read as key-works in a video.  
* *AI-Artist* (August 13 \- 28th, 2024\) [https://glia.ca/2024/ai-artist/](https://glia.ca/2024/ai-artist/): initiated 4 days after Sakana released version 1 of their *AI Scientist[^3]*. Output: 10 minute video-lecture.  
* *Messages to Humanity* (Nov 2024\) [https://glia.ca/2024/messages/](https://glia.ca/2024/messages/) : “On Oct 31st, one week after the release of [Runway's Act-One](https://runwayml.com/research/introducing-act-one), I decided to make and release one brief video per day for 30 days. Investigating AI's evolving creative potential to create micro-parable messages appropriate in a disintegrative era. 'Messages to Humanity' began on Oct 31st, 2024, and concluded (on schedule, as planned) after 30 videos on Nov 29th, 2024.”
